Child Safety & CSAE Protection Standards

Last Updated: 2025

1. Introduction

Our application is committed to protecting all users—especially children—from any form of child sexual abuse and exploitation (CSAE). We maintain a strict zero-tolerance policy toward Child Sexual Abuse Material (CSAM) and any harmful interaction involving minors.

2. Legal Compliance and Standards

We align with internationally recognized safety frameworks:

  • Local and international child protection laws
  • WeProtect Global Alliance — Global Framework
  • Tech Coalition — Project Protect Principles
  • NCMEC reporting standards
  • INHOPE global reporting networks

3. Prohibited Content and Behaviors

3.1 Child Sexual Abuse Material (CSAM)

  • Any media depicting sexual content involving minors
  • AI-generated or manipulated sexualized images of minors
  • Sexualized messages involving children

3.2 Child Abuse and Neglect

  • Physical, emotional, or psychological abuse
  • Manipulation, grooming, threats, coercive behavior

3.3 Safety Risks

  • Requests for personal information from minors
  • Inappropriate communication with minors

4. Detection Technologies and Procedures

4.1 Automated Detection

  • Hash-matching systems (e.g., PhotoDNA)
  • ML-based harmful content detection
  • Real-time upload scanning

4.2 Human Review

  • Trained safety reviewers
  • 24/7 emergency escalation

4.3 Privacy Protection

  • Systems used strictly for safety purposes only
  • Minimal data access principles

5. Reporting and Enforcement

5.1 User Reporting Tools

Users can report:

  • Harmful content
  • Suspicious interactions
  • Urgent child safety concerns

5.2 Enforcement Actions

  • Immediate content removal
  • Account suspension or permanent ban
  • Mandatory reporting to law enforcement when required
  • Reporting to NCMEC or national authorities

6. Preventive Safety Measures

  • Pre-upload safety filtering
  • Risky account behavior monitoring
  • Enhanced protection for underage users
  • Safety guidance for parents and youth

7. Transparency and Auditing

We publish annual transparency reports outlining CSAE-related data, enforcement activity, and system improvements. We also undergo third-party audits to maintain compliance.
